Overview

Created a platform dedicated to conserving endangered species of whales by raising awareness and inspiring change. Project goals were to:

• Create a mobile app
• Turn the mobile app into a responsive website.

Project Name

Save The Whales

Duration

8 weeks

Role

User Experience and User Interface Designer

Tools Used

Figma, Google Workspace

Empathize

Conducted surveys to figure out use cases, functionalities and expectations for mobile app and website. Utilized the data from 10 participants to create personas and user stories.

Thea Wood Persona

Thea is an active fighter when it comes to saving endangered species of whales. She wants to have access to reliable information and wants to know what she can do on a daily basis to help out.

User Needs and Use Cases

“ List of products that we use in our daily lives that can be harmful to sea creatures for me to avoid... Things that I can do easily on the day to day. ”

“ I would like if there is any feature or donation or any help we could make to help the people who are working on saving these endangered species. ”


Through the personas and user stories, I was able to figure out the use cases along with user needs.

For the app:
As the app will be used on the go and for specific tasks in mind, the use case will be for keeping track of daily activities that can be done to help increase biodiversity in the ocean.

For the website:
The website will be directed more towards getting users to donate and adopt, as well as more in depth research into related current events.

Defining the Problem

Currently users lack the time and effort to conduct research and keep track of daily actions. They want a platform that contains the information they need but will also keep them accountable and motivated.

Ideate

How might we create an app that will inform and inspire change?

How might we create a trustworthy platform that will work towards fixing common problems in the field?

Competitive Audit

Conducted an audit on three apps dedicated to conserving endangered animals. The audit was based on 4 categories; information offered, features, navigation, and overall effectiveness. From my audit, it revealed that current apps don't have the right balance between inspiring users to take action and intuitive design.

Wireframes

After research and defining the problem, the app and website were visualized through the creation of wireframes. Turned the wireframes into low fidelity prototypes to conduct a usability study.

Prototype and Test

Conducted a usability study on 5 participants. Main insights of the usability study were:

1) Accounts page was overcrowded. Needed more white space and a better layout.

2) There was no way to reset the progress tracker. Needed a way to reset.

3) Navigation was not accessible during the entire user flow. Needed consistent and clear navigation throughout.

Designing the Website

Converted the mobile app into a responsive website, keeping in mind that the website will be used for tasks that require a longer time to complete such as in depth research, donations and adoptions.

Design System

Created a system to design efficiently and to keep platforms consistent. Contained multiple components, some of which include color scheme, buttons, navigation and text.

Next Steps

• More research on current methods of conservation to implement other useful features on the app.

• More user research to expand use cases for website.

• Test responsive website.

What I learned

Quality research leads to a successful product.The beginning stages of defining user needs, motivations, expectations, and clearly outlining the problems is essential because every step afterward relies on the research that was done.
